2. Data processing

TechnoDaily is designed with a 100% on-device processing approach. Here is what data the application handles and where it is stored:

The application fetches public RSS feeds that you configure. Articles and their metadata (title, summary, images) are stored locally on your device using SwiftData (Apple's native database framework).

You can revoke this access at any time in the app Settings or via your Google account permissions.

2.3 YouTube connection (subscriptions)

If you choose to connect your YouTube account, the application uses Google OAuth 2.0 for read-only access to your subscriptions and recent videos. Same principle: authentication occurs between your device and Google. Data is stored locally.

2.4 Reading preferences and scores

The application analyzes your reading habits (articles read, preferred sources, reading times) to personalize your recommendations through a scoring algorithm. This processing is performed entirely on your device by your iPhone/iPad processor. No behavioral data is sent to any external server.

2.5 Smart summaries (Apple Intelligence)

If your device supports it, TechnoDaily uses Apple Intelligence (Apple's on-device AI framework) to generate article summaries. This processing happens entirely on your device, with no data sent to third-party servers.

2.6 iCloud sync (CloudKit)

If you have iCloud enabled on your device, your TechnoDaily data (articles, preferences, configured feeds) may be synchronized across your Apple devices via CloudKit. This synchronization is managed by Apple according to their own privacy policy. Techno&Co has no access to your iCloud account or synchronized data.

Techno&Co does not own or operate any servers. The application runs entirely on your device. The only network connections are:
